Technical Crash Investigation

September 16-27, 2019

NLETC

This course will be eighty-hours (80) in length and emphasis will be on “hands-on” accident investigation. Students will do technical roadway and vehicle examination, and forensic examinations. The physics of vehicle dynamics and occupant kinematics will be studied. The students will do vehicle speed determinations to include: linear momentum and time distance calculations and applications. Outside testing projects will also be included.

Crash Reconstruction

Late Fall 2019

NLETC

This 80 hour course will finalize a student's progress to become an accident reconstructionist. Upon completion of this course the student will be able to fully reconstruct a crash through the use of the skills learned in these courses as well as further on-road experience. A continuation of topics taught in our Technical course will further the student's knowledge and experience in the following areas of emphasis.

  • Derivations of crash investigation formulas
  • Using the conservation of linear momentum (one and two dimensional)
  • Further discussion and examples of vehicle dynamics
  • Special problem cases involving unique vehicles and situations
  • Full projects from evidence organization, analytics and final opinions created through the use of data and physical evidence gathered at the scene.
